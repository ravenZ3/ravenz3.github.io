I do not know exactly where I am going. I have made peace with that, partially.

What I know is the texture of the life I am building toward - not the destination, but the feeling of moving correctly. There is a difference between being lost and being in motion without a fixed address. I am the latter, I think. Most days.

---

## On Freedom

The deepest thing I want is to not be trapped. Not by birthplace, not by finances, not by someone else's structure, not by my own fear. I want the ability to leave weak conditions, to reinvent, to choose my environment rather than inherit it by default.

This is not wanderlust. It is something more serious - existential mobility as a core value. The ability to pick up and go, literally and metaphorically, is worth more to me than almost any specific destination.

Chennai is not a limitation. It is current terrain. All places are current terrain.

---

## On Work

I want to build things that did not exist before. Intelligent systems, music, writing, maybe companies. The act of making is how I process being alive - not as productivity, not as output, but as thinking made visible.

I want ownership over what I build and how I build it. Not because I am arrogant - I have plenty to learn from everyone - but because I think in ways that need room. Tight structures do not make me more productive. They make me smaller.

I am drawn to hard problems. ML pipelines that don't converge, sensors that lie, models that fail on edge cases. I like the moment when the system reveals something unexpected and you have to sit with it and figure out what it is actually telling you. That is when I feel most awake.

I do not need a prestigious address on my resume to know I am capable. But I am not yet fully free of that need. That is honest.

---

## On the Body

I want to be physically alive, not just mentally. Surfing, calisthenics, gymnastics - these are not hobbies appended to a career. They are the same philosophy applied to a different medium. Mastery through your own body. No equipment, no institution, no permission required. Portable. Honest. Difficult in the right ways.

The body and mind are not separate departments. I refuse to live like they are.

---

## On Curiosity

I cannot help being interested in too many things. Neuroscience, quantum physics, embedded systems, philosophy, music theory, competitive programming. People sometimes read this as scattered. I read it as integrity.

I suspect the most interesting things live at the intersections. The person who only knows one domain deeply is useful. The person who moves between domains fluidly and finds the connections - that person sees things others cannot.

I want to understand how things actually work. Not the surface explanation. The real one underneath.

---

## On Creating

I want to write a book someday. I want to play guitar in a street somewhere that is not here. I want to make music that is mine. I want to write code that solves something real for someone real.

Creating is not separate from living. It is the evidence that I was here and paying attention.

---

## On Needing Little

I do not need much to feel rich. Good problems, a guitar, open water somewhere, people worth talking to, enough money to not be anxious. That is the list. It is shorter than most people's and I think that is an advantage, not a lack.

Low burn rate is a weapon. I am learning to use it.

---

## On Recognition and Proof

I want to prove something. I know this about myself and I am not fully sure who I am proving it to. Myself, probably. The version of me that still isn't sure.

I am working on making that need smaller. Not eliminating it - it is fuel - but making it a servant rather than a master. The goal is to one day not need the external verdict. To just know.

I am not there yet. That is fine.

---

## On the Whole Thing

I am trying to become someone who is deeply competent but not institutional. Fully alive, not just successful. Creative without performing creativity. Free without being unmoored.

The Stark-Parker thing, someone once reflected back to me. Builds alone in a room, figures it out, but also feels things deeply and wants to actually live. That landed.

I am not trying to become someone unrecognizable. I am trying to become more fully myself. That is the whole project.

It will take longer than I want. It will not go according to plan because there is no plan, only direction. The direction is - more free, more capable, more alive, more honest.

That is enough to move toward.

---

*Written at 22, in Chennai, when things were unclear but starting to have a shape.*

*Unfinished. Deliberately. The best thing that can happen to this is that time proves it wrong in interesting ways.*
